\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

The two invariants cover two disjoint failure modes, the susceptibility measurement exposes where the residual
margin lies, and the leak-aware protocol fixes what the numbers can and cannot claim. We interpret the mechanism,
outline the practical implications, and state the limits.

\subsection{Interpretation: two invariants, two failure modes}
The two invariants are complementary responses to two disjoint modes of targeted attack
(Sec.~\ref{sec:res-inv1},~\ref{sec:res-inv2}). The per-threat ablation makes the complementarity concrete: for
new senders the signal is carried by OSINT and time is useless, and for compromises it is the reverse. This is
why single approaches fail on the residual $2\%$ (a content filter, a contact graph, or a volumetric detector on
its own): each is blind to one of the two modes.

A second result concerns the stealthy attacker. A volumetric detector in the spirit of COMPA
\citep{stringhini2015thataint} assumes that a compromise shows up as a surge; when the attacker deliberately
spreads the sends over time, the volumetric signal vanishes and its AUC drops below the random baseline. Cascade dynamics instead
reads the recipient side, the wave-like signature among the neighbors, which the sender cannot hide. This agrees
with Ho et al.\ \citep{ho2019detecting} that lateral phishing needs a signal beyond the content of a single
message; we differ in locating that signal in the dynamics of the domain knowledge graph rather than in headers
or relays.

\subsection{Practical implications}
The central finding from Sec.~\ref{sec:susceptibility} --- that the periphery, not the executives, is most
exposed under a lateral threat --- runs counter to common practice and is the most concrete takeaway of the
paper. Typical defense programs treat executives as the most exposed, yet for the lateral channel the opposite
holds: the largest unaddressed margin lies on the periphery, the mid- and junior-level roles. The recommendation
is two-part. First, awareness programs and hard controls, such as out-of-band verification for transfers, should
cover the periphery and not only the top. Second, detection resources are worth directing where the domain
knowledge graph is sparse, since that is where the residual margin is largest. For the cold impersonation
channel no relational layer suffices ($\mathrm{Susc}\!\approx\!0.96$ regardless of rank), so here the graph must
be combined with content and process controls, as the fusion experiment (Sec.~\ref{sec:res-content}) confirms.
The domain knowledge graph is therefore a prioritization layer rather than a standalone product: it indicates
who, and through which relationship, is most weakly protected.

The result also bears on the human factor. Human susceptibility rises under time pressure and authority cues
while awareness training moderates it only partially (Section~\ref{sec:related}), so a structural susceptibility
measure allows limited training interventions to target the roles the technology protects least.

\subsection{Limitations}
The limitations define the scope of the claims. \textit{First, maliciousness is synthetic.} No public corpus
links real OSINT facts with labeled compromises, so we generate attack labels on real structure (Enron, SNAP) or
on a synthetic population of twins. We therefore validate the mechanism (out-of-rhythm mail separates
compromises; layer inconsistency separates impersonation), not the detection of real intrusions. The OSINT
multiplex advantage of Invariant~I remains purely synthetic, and we read it as a proof of soundness rather than a
measurement on real traffic. There is also some circularity: the synthetic population is generated graph-first,
so detecting a violation of that structure is partly built in. In line with this, the layer analysis
(\ref{sec:res-inv1}) shows the temporal layer carrying almost all of Invariant~I's signal, with the structural
and OSINT layers contributing little unless the topology is artificially decorrelated; the headline of
Invariant~I then reduces to rhythm consistency detecting off-rhythm mail, whereas the cascade result
(Invariant~II) establishes the receiving-side signal much more robustly on real graphs. \textit{Second, the
susceptibility measurement inherits these assumptions.} The $\mathrm{Susc}$ figures are relative to our event
model and population structure (19~companies of about 8 people, one domain per company); the direction of the
conclusions (relationship over rank; periphery most exposed) is robust, but the exact values do not
transfer directly to every organization. The direction is also partly definitional: executives are layer-rich by
construction, so their stronger structural protection follows in part from how we tied layer richness to
seniority, a coupling that should be stress-tested under alternative assignments before the periphery-first
recommendation is generalized. \textit{Third, the scale is limited} ($N{=}150$ twins; real graphs up to about 2k
nodes), so the comparison with heavy GNN architectures (GATNE/HGT) is a sanity check rather than a full benchmark.
\textit{Fourth, the operational threshold.} Even the best operating point (sensitivity@FPR$1\%$) is orders of
magnitude from the deployment threshold on real mail (Ho et al.\ \citep{ho2019detecting}: $<\!4$~FP/M); we report
it as a requirement we are beginning to meet rather than a record.

\subsection{Future work directions}
The most important next step is validation on a corpus with labeled, real compromises, turning the proof of
mechanism into a measurement of detectability. On the modeling side, the hand-crafted consistency features could
give way to a learned multiplex GNN with explicit cross-layer consistency regularization, validated inductively
on unseen organizations. The threat model itself should then be stressed: an attacker that \emph{learns} the
domain knowledge graph (LLM~$+$~OSINT \citep{bethany2024evaluating}) and tunes rhythm and relationships, together
with population drift over time (TESSERACT \citep{pendlebury2019tesseract}). Finally, the measured $\mathrm{Susc}$
is a natural steering signal for dynamic prioritization of controls and personalization of training, provided an
evaluation ties it to a real reduction in click-through.
